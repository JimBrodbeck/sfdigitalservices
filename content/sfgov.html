A single website for San Franciscans

We’re developing a new website that will make it easier for San Franciscans to get a birth certificate, register a business, and get a marriage license. It’s not meant to represent the 300+ websites the City currently has, but to serve all San Franciscans, simply and directly.

Many other big cities and even the UK government are doing this too—bringing services together based on resident’s needs and putting them first, with services that are clear, simple, and easy to use so people can do they things we know they want to.

Feedback and insight from more than 300 residents, community groups and City staff informs our design, testing, and changes. We’ve heard:


  • The new website “...should be like Starbucks. I don’t need the best coffee, but I know what I’m buying and I’m out in 5 minutes.”
  • “The City of SF should have a website that reflects its own personality”



Our design principles are to


  • Represent the City’s diversity
  • Celebrate our unique culture and progressive values
  • Be accessible and inclusive for all people
  • Reflect that the website is reliable and easy to use
  • Be flexible for the variety of content we offer

How we’re living up to those principles

Our City seal shows residents that they are on an official City site. Residents have told us that helps them know they are in the right place.

San Franciscans told us that the equal sign hidden in the SF symbol reflects our progressive values.

There are few photos because we don’t want to use up San Franciscan’s data plan on images.

A wide range of colors mirrors diversity with a scalable color palette.

Color blocks keep visuals fresh without relying on photos.

Our font is clear and legible and legally compliant with ADA and Section 508 for people with disabilities.

Translations

Every transaction page and department homepage will be translated into Chinese (traditional), Spanish, and Filipino by humans. All other pages will be translated by machine.

We’re working with the Language Access group on automatic, high-quality translations. Departments won’t need to translate.

We will always test the translations and the site with people who don’t speak English as their first language.

Writing

A 5th grade reading level means it’s easy for everyone to read and translate.

Our writing guidelines ensure consistency and simplicity across the site, so screenreaders and people can read faster.



Where we are now

We’re working with a few departments to move their services, information, news, events and department homepages to the new website. We’ll build more pieces of the site soon, but for right now we’re focused on services for San Franciscans. We have prioritized departments that offer services and also provided us insight from all levels of staff.

Move to the new site

  • the title of your department
  • a summary of what your department does (under 110 characters)
