Project Coordinator

About the team

The San Francisco Digital Services Team is creating world class digital services. By putting the needs of our residents first and using modern software development practices we are making it easy to for residents, businesses, and visitors to interact with the City and get things done online.

We are a new team that is expanding rapidly. We are a group of designers, developers, and product managers who work in multi-disciplinary teams to design public services and develop technology that makes them easy to use. We are passionate about user-centered design and improving public services in San Francisco.

Our team values are:

  • Build with San Franciscans
  • Be honest with everyone
  • Help each other be successful
  • Work hard, laugh often
  • More making, less talking

We have two priorities for the next year: To completely redesign the city’s website from the ground up; and to redesign permitting for the city. No small tasks, these two priorities need a laser focus and determination to make them happen. In the process of achieving these goals, we will be developing technical, design, and content standards that can be shared and adopted widely across the city.

As we grow our team, we are looking for a Project Coordinator with exceptional skills to help us get organized and do things professionally and efficiently. In particular, we need someone to help us make sure that we are testing everything we do with the residents of San Francisco.

Salary range: $80,912 - $98,358/year
Based at 1231 Stevenson Street
Reporting to Deputy Chief Digital Services Officer

About the role

This role requires an administrative superhero. Someone who has attention to detail and who is incredibly well organized. You will be responsible for all administration in the office and for making sure that the team has everything it needs to work at its best. Tasks will include:

  • Research community organizations and develop a database of those we can work with for user testing. Develop relationships with them to enable user research.
  • Organize and coordinate user research sessions, ensuring that rooms are booked, records are kept, participants sign waivers, incentives are distributed, participants are welcomed or otherwise facilitated.
  • Help us analyze the results of user testing and recommend improvements to the technology we are building. Also be willing to learn how to conduct user testing and eventually conduct user testing sessions yourself.
  • Keep on top of all of our contracts and software licenses and make sure our paperwork is in order. Write reports on our procurement and help us find ways to save money.
  • Organize team meetings, outings, and events from coming up with ideas, to taking care of logistics, to making sure everything runs smoothly on the day.
  • Help us with recruitment, from making sure job adverts are posted widely, to helping us organize interviews, to making sure that we can follow HR processes.
  • Sometimes help us test the new software we make, to make sure that it works properly.
  • Work with the managers on the team to make sure that they are doing all of their team management duties and help them stay organized with their management tasks (e.g. scheduling 1-1 meetings)
  • Research and analyze new technologies and new regulations to make sure that we are following best practice and staying current with technologies.
  • Document everything. Once we document our current processes (user testing, procurement, hiring, reimbursements, etc.), propose ideas to improve the way we do things.
  • Finally, have fun! This is a fast-paced, nontraditional government team where we get stuff done! You will play an important role in everything we do.
